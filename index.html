What internships you should apply to? Apply to an internship that offers a position relevant to your major. Internships will either offer you paid or college credit. Do not base your decision on that. Choose something that will make better you as a person and professional.


Resume:
Your resume should begin with a concise summary of who you are, what your interest are, and it must be relevant to the internship you are applying to. Your resume list your achievements, but do not sound too self centered. People value that you are a people’s person, and an innovative minded individual. Use bullet point. The employer should be able to skim your resume and understand what you are all about. Avoid being Chatty. Formal language and correct usage of mechanics and spelling. Research the company whose internship application you are applying to. Knowing what their vision and priorities are will help you adjust your resume accordingly. Be yourself. Do not lie about who you are because it will only make it harder for you if you do get hired as an intern. Stating what you believe,what your interest are, and what your visions are make you an attractive applicant. Kept it concise.

Before the interview: Send out an email asking questions left unanswered. However, don’t ask questions you can just look up at their website. Do your research. You will feel prepared and it will increase your confidence. Talking will come naturally. Prepare for unexpected. Write down questions the interviewer might ask you. Practise answering them. Several employers like to ask random questions that might not make sense, so prepare to improvise and be comfortable with it. Go to sleep early, wake up early, eat breakfast, and prepare for traffic. Create a checklist of tasks you must complete before the interview.


Interview: Keep cool because you got this. Be on time. Show enthusiasm and engagement. Dress appropriately. It is better to look professional. As they ask you questions, give examples of your experiences relevant to the question. Ask questions. It shows that you are interested and want to know more. After the interview, send out a thank you email and concisely remind them why they should chose you. Do not beg for the position. Keep it short and simple.


How to make the best out of your internship? Companies love it when original ideas have a potential to make their company better. Don’t be afraid to make suggestions, contribute in the pool of ideas, and establish a good relationships with your coworkers. Helping your peers grow will not only make you feel good, but it moves the team forward. They will be there when you need them. Stay calm and carry on.


Keep in mind: There are other applicants that are competing for the same limited position as you. The more outstanding you appear in your application, the more likely you will get the spot. Apply to a few internships. You are more than likely to get accepted to at least one position. Trust yourself to make the right decision because you will. So go out there, work hard, and build your empire one step at a time; Skill, will, and determination. :)
